8-question template - claude_code SPA

1. What problem does the application solve? - Allows parents to monitor and regulate their child's screen time regarding specific apps.

2. Who are the users? - Parents are the main users. Children are the users signed up by the parents.

3. What are their primary goals? - To regulate screentime on specific apps for children. Parents can set parameters for how long their child can be on a certain app (be it a game, search engine, or social media). Parents can also use the app to set a password confirmation from them (the parent) before the child accesses the app. Restrict access to applications that need a parent's password before the child uses it.

4. What information do they need? - Parent email is required, recommended biometrics set up, but not required. Password is required, username is required. It is not required to put in the child's age. A username for the child is required, so parents can easily set up parameters for each individual child. Screentime data will be required, as well as access to monitoring certain apps. Access to location, photos, or phone numbers/contacts is not required. The app WILL NOT look at search history. The app will track how much time a child spent on each app, breaking up their screen time into increments. 

5. What actions can they perform? - Send notifications to parents when a child is attempting to access an app without the parent's permission, stop the application from running when the child's time runs out (there will be a five minute warning issued prior to the forced stop). Restricted access to apps for the child, pending parental approval. Monitoring of screentime, and app time of users.

6. What pages are required? - sign up and login page, homepage, profile page, screentime history (listing out the combined time online, breaking it up into the apps the child spent time on. For instance, it might say 8 hours, and list 2 hours in Instagram, 3 hours on YouTube, and 2.5 hours on Minecraft, and half an hour on google)

7. What data must be collected? - Screentime, time user spent on specific applications. Parental approval for child's access to certain apps. 

8. What permissions exist? - Privacy data related to monitoring of how long children/users interact with specific applications. Parent email, parent password.
